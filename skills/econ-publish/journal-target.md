# /journal-target — Recommend target journals and submission strategy

## Trigger
"where should I submit?", "journal recommendations", "what journal fits this paper?"

## Workflow

### Step 1: Assess the paper
Read the manuscript and identify:
1. **Field**: IO, urban, labor, macro, applied micro, etc.
2. **Method**: RCT, DiD, RDD, IV, structural, descriptive
3. **Finding type**: Strong positive, null, ambiguous, methodological
4. **Data scale**: Small/medium/large
5. **Policy relevance**: High/medium/low
6. **Author career stage**: PhD student, junior faculty, senior

### Step 2: Match to journals

**Top-5 general (hardest):**
| Journal | Best for | Desk reject risk |
|---|---|---|
| AER | Field-defining contributions | Very high |
| Econometrica | Methodological innovations | Very high |
| QJE | Big questions, clean designs | Very high |
| JPE | Strong identification, IO/labor | Very high |
| REStud | Theory + empirics | Very high |

### Step 3: Career-stage-specific advice

**First paper (assistant professor, no publications):**
- Goal: GET PUBLISHED. A published EL > a RAND desk reject.
- Strategy: Two-track (short letter + full field journal)
- Aim for medium-probability targets (30-50% acceptance odds)
- Do NOT spend 2 years in revision purgatory at a top journal
- Time limit: one R&R cycle (8-9 months max) before pivoting

**Established faculty (2+ publications):**
- Can afford to aim higher and absorb desk rejections
- Top-5 field journals are realistic targets
- R&R signals are valuable even if ultimately rejected

**PhD student:**
- Prioritize getting a working paper circulating (SSRN, NBER)
- Target journals where your advisor has connections
- EL submission fee is reduced to $67.50 for students

### Step 4: Dual-track protocol

**When it's ethical:**
- Short version (letter) + long version (full paper) = different papers
- Different length, scope, contribution claim, audience
- MUST disclose in cover letter to the second journal

**When it's NOT ethical:**
- Same paper to two journals simultaneously
- Same length/scope with minor reframing

**Disclosure language:**
"A shorter version focusing solely on [X finding] is under review at *[Journal]*. The present manuscript extends the analysis to include [Y, Z] and differs in scope and contribution."

### Step 5: Submission timeline template

| Week | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Submit short version (EL or similar) |
| 1-4 | Prepare full version for field journal |
| 5 | Submit full version |
| 6-10 | EL desk decision expected |
| 12-20 | Field journal decision expected |
| If reject | Revise and resubmit to next target within 2 weeks |
