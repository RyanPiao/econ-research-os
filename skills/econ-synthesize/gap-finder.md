# /gap-finder — Systematic Research Gap Identification

## Inputs
- `--scan ./literature/` to scan all paper snapshots
- Or `--topic [topic]` to filter by tag
- Or explicit list of files

## Procedure

### Step 1: Extract gap data from snapshots
For each paper snapshot in scope, read the "Gap Analysis" section (generated by /read-paper):
- Boundary gaps (where the claim stops)
- Mechanism gaps (effect exists but why?)
- Measurement gaps (concept measured weakly)
- Design gaps (identification could be cleaner)

### Step 2: Aggregate across papers
Group gaps by type. Within each type, identify:
- **Recurring gaps**: mentioned by 3+ papers → high-value research opportunity
- **Unique gaps**: mentioned by only 1 paper → may be idiosyncratic or novel
- **Contradictory gaps**: Paper A says "need more X" but Paper B already did X → check if B actually addressed it

### Step 3: Rank by feasibility
For each gap, assess:
- Data availability: can you get the data to fill this gap?
- Identification feasibility: is there a credible design for this question?
- Novelty: has anyone else already started filling this gap? (check forward citations)
- Relevance to your project: does filling this gap serve YOUR paper/book?

### Step 4: Generate actionable next steps
For the top 3–5 gaps, translate into concrete research actions:
- "Collect [specific data] from [source] to test [claim] in [new setting]"
- "Run [robustness check] that [paper] didn't do"
- "Apply [method] from [paper A] to [data] from [paper B]"
- "Test [mechanism] that [paper] proposed but didn't empirically validate"
