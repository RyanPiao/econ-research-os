# /cover-letter — Draft journal cover letter

## Trigger
"write cover letter for [journal]", "draft EL cover letter"

## Template

```markdown
[Date]

The Editor
*[Journal Name]*

Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit "[Paper Title]" for consideration at *[Journal Name]*.

[1-2 sentences: What the paper does and the main finding.]

[1-2 sentences: Why it's timely — cite relevant policy events, recent publications, or ongoing debates.]

[1 sentence: Why this journal is the right venue — connect to the journal's scope.]

[If dual-track: "A [shorter/longer] version [focusing on X / extending the analysis to Y] is [under review at / in preparation for] *[Other Journal]*. The present manuscript differs in [scope/length/contribution]."]

The manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere [except as noted above].

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,

[Author Name]
[Title]
[Department, University]
[Email]
[Website]
```

## Journal-specific framing

### Economics Letters
- Lead with the focused finding (one sentence)
- Emphasize timeliness (DOJ, FTC, policy moment)
- Note it's a "short communication" suited to EL's format
- Keep to 3-4 sentences total

### Journal of Urban Economics
- Lead with urban/housing framing ("how platform pricing tools affect STR markets")
- Emphasize city-level heterogeneity as a feature
- Connect to housing policy debates
- If EL version exists: disclose as "a shorter version focusing solely on the price-level finding"

### RAND / AEJ:Micro
- Lead with identification strategy and clean contribution
- Emphasize novelty relative to Assad et al.
- Note the policy relevance but don't overstate
- These editors care about rigor, not policy advocacy

### JIE / IJIO
- Lead with the market-structure-mediates-effects framing
- Position as complement to Assad et al.
- Emphasize careful null with honest limitations
- JIE appreciates modesty

## Rules
- Never exceed one page
- No technical details (the paper speaks for itself)
- No begging ("I hope you will consider...")
- Author info at bottom, not top
- If suggesting reviewers, put in a separate "suggested reviewers" field, not the cover letter
